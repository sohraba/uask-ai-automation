    Applying for a new Emirates ID card is a mandatory process for all UAE citizens, residents, and even newborns. Below is a clear, step-by-step guide to help you through the application process, including required documents, application channels, and what to expect after submission.

    ---

    ### 1. Prepare the Required Documents

    The documents you need depend on your status:

    | Category | Required Documents |
    |----------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
    | UAE Nationals | - Original passport<br>- Birth certificate (for first-time applicants)<br>- Passport-size photo |
    | GCC Nationals | - Valid passport<br>- Valid GCC ID card<br>- Proof of residency (e.g., trade licence, study certificate)<br>- Passport-size photo |
    | Expatriate Residents | - Valid passport with residence permit<br>- Proof of residence (e.g., tenancy contract, utility bill)<br>- Passport-size photo |
    | Children under 15 | - Original birth certificate<br>- Passports of both parents<br>- Passport-size photo of the child |

    > Note: For newborns, you may also need the sponsor’s passport, visa, and Emirates ID (original)[^5].

    ---

    ### 2. Choose Your Application Channel

    You can apply for a new Emirates ID through any of the following methods:

    - Online via the Federal Authority for Identity, Citizenship, Customs and Ports Security (ICP) website or ICP mobile app.
    - At an accredited typing centre.
    - At Amer Centres (especially in Dubai) for in-person assistance[^2][^5].

    ---

    ### 3. Complete the Application Form

    - Fill out the Emirates ID application form online or at the chosen centre.
    - Ensure all information is accurate to avoid delays.

    ---

    ### 4. Submit Your Documents

    - Submit the required documents along with your application.
    - The staff will verify your documents before proceeding[^5].

    ---

    ### 5. Pay the Applicable Fees

    - Fees vary based on the validity period (typically 1, 2, or 3 years) and your category.
    - Example (subject to change):
    - 1 year: ~AED 359.75
    - 2 years: ~AED 459.75
    - 3 years: ~AED 559.75
    - Urgent processing: Additional AED 79[^5]
    - Fees may differ for partners’ families and other categories.

    ---

    ### 6. Provide Biometric Data (if Required)

    - First-time applicants and some renewals may need to visit an ICP service centre to provide fingerprints and a photo[^2].

    ---

    ### 7. Track Your Application

    - After submission, you will receive an SMS notification regarding your application status.
    - You can also track your application online or through the ICP app[^2][^5].

    ---

    ### 8. Collect Your Emirates ID

    - Once your ID is ready, you will receive an SMS with collection instructions.
    - Collect your Emirates ID from the designated Emirates Post office or the centre where you applied[^2][^5].

    ---

    ## Additional Tips

    - The processing time is usually between 24 hours (urgent) and 5 days (standard), but this may vary[^5].
    - Always keep your Emirates ID safe, as it is required for many official transactions in the UAE.
    - For the latest updates or assistance, you can contact the ICP Call Centre at 6005 30003 or visit your nearest Amer Centre[^2][^5].
